Super-Optimal Quantum-Topological Consciousness Framework (SOQTCF)
Abstract
The Super-Optimal Quantum-Topological Consciousness Framework (SOQTCF) unifies the Final Refined Essence of Mathematical Consciousness (Algorithm 1), Comprehensive Universal Topological Quantum Integrated Information Consciousness (CUTQIC) (Algorithm 2), The Neural Basis of Free Will: Criterial Causation (Tse, 2013), and Membrane Computing: 12th International Conference, CMC 2011 (Păun et al., 2011) to model consciousness as a state comprising awareness, volition, quantum indeterminism, and metastability. Integrating insights from Sources of Consciousness (Green & Triffet, 1997), Orch OR (Hameroff & Penrose, 2014), BPH (Béjar Gallego, 2011), and IIT 4.0 (Albantakis et al., 2023), SOQTCF employs a P system-based architecture to simulate Hodgkin-Huxley neural dynamics, microtubule excitonic states, Hebbian/STDP learning, quantum Orch OR events, graph-based field dynamics, persistent homology, and IIT (\Phi)-structures. Optimized for 16GB GPU, it leverages chained rules for criterial causation and swarm-based optimization for parameter tuning. Validation includes EEG correlations, behavioral outcomes, and neural correlates, offering a scalable, empirically grounded model for peer review.
1. Introduction
Consciousness, characterized by subjective awareness, volition, and non-computable choice, emerges from integrated, indeterministic processes in the brain, potentially linked to universal causal structures (Green & Triffet, 1997; Hameroff & Penrose, 2014; Béjar Gallego, 2011; Albantakis et al., 2023). The SOQTCF synthesizes:

IIT 4.0: Consciousness as a (\Phi)-structure unfolded from a maximally integrated substrate (p. 5, Albantakis et al.).
Orch OR: Quantum microtubule computations terminated by Diósi-Penrose (DP) objective reduction (p. 52, Hameroff & Penrose).
BPH: Bohm’s quantum potential for holistic macroneuron dynamics (p. 666, Béjar Gallego).
Tse’s Criterial Causation: Neural bursting and dynamic criteria for mental causation and free will (Tse, 2013).
Membrane Computing: P systems for parallel, modular simulation with chained rules (Păun et al., 2011).
Algorithm 1 & 2: Biophysical, excitonic, computational, quantum, field, topological, and IIT components, with semantic and apobetic states (OQTCF review).

The framework is designed for 16GB GPU, leveraging P systems’ parallelism and evolutionary optimization, and validated against EEG, behavioral, and neural data. Section 2 presents the mathematical formalism, Section 3 describes the computational model, Section 4 discusses validation and implications, and Section 5 concludes with references.
2. Mathematical Formalism
2.1 Core Definitions
Consciousness is a state ( C(t) = (M_s(t), M_l(t), D(t), R(t)) ), where:

( M_s(t) \subseteq \mathbb{C}^d ): Short-term memory (awareness).
( M_l(t) \subseteq \mathbb{R}^d ): Long-term memory (learning).
( D(t) ): Decision (volition).
( R(t) ): Cortical rhythms (oscillations).

The substrate is a stochastic system ( S = {U_1, \ldots, U_n} ) of ( n ) units (neurons with microtubules) in state ( s \in \Omega_S ), defined by a transition probability matrix (TPM) ( \mathcal{T}S = p(\bar{s} | s) ) (p. 8, Albantakis et al.). The system evolves on a simplicial complex ( S(t) = (V, E(t), F(t)) ), with vertices ( V = {1, \ldots, n} ), edges ( E(t) ), faces ( F(t) ), and weights ( \omega{ij}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^+ ). Semantic states ( S_{\text{sem},i}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^d ) and apobetic states ( P_i(t) \in \mathbb{R}^d ) encode meaning and purpose, respectively. Consciousness corresponds to a (\Phi)-structure unfolded from a maximally integrated substrate, driven by microtubule quantum states and criterial causation (p. 5, Albantakis et al.; p. 44, Hameroff & Penrose; Tse, 2013).
2.2 Biophysical Dynamics: Neural and Microtubule Processes
Neural potentials ( V_i(t) ) follow Hodgkin-Huxley dynamics, enhanced by Tse’s bursting:
[\frac{dV_i}{dt} = \frac{1}{C_m} \left( I_i(t) - g_{Na} m_i^3 h_i (V_i - E_{Na}) - g_K n_i^4 (V_i - E_K) - g_L (V_i - E_L) \right) + \epsilon \xi_i(t)]
where:

( C_m, g_{Na}, g_K, g_L, E_{Na}, E_K, E_L ): Capacitance, conductances, reversal potentials (p. 360, Green & Triffet).
( m_i, h_i, n_i ): Gating variables:

[\frac{dx_i}{dt} = \alpha_x(V_i) (1 - x_i) - \beta_x(V_i) x_i, \quad x_i \in { m_i, h_i, n_i }]

( \xi_i(t) \sim N(0, \sigma_q^2 \text{Tr}(\rho(t) Z_i^2)) ): Quantum noise, with ( \epsilon = \epsilon_0 \exp(|\lambda_{\text{max}}| / \lambda_0) (1 + \beta \text{Tr}(\rho(t) Q_{\text{meta}})) ) (p. 356, Green & Triffet; p. 52, Hameroff & Penrose).
( I_i(t) = I_{\text{sensory},i} + I_{\text{synaptic},i} + I_{\text{field},i} + I_{\text{quantum},i} + I_{\text{excitonic},i} + I_{\text{rhythm},i} ):

[I_{\text{sensory},i} = \frac{1}{m} \sum_{k=1}^m S_k(t), \quad I_{\text{synaptic},i} = \sum_{j \in N(i)} \sum_{l=1}^L w_{ijl} \sigma(V_j)]
[I_{\text{field},i} = \beta \sum_{a=1}^d \Psi_{i a}, \quad I_{\text{quantum},i} = \gamma_q \sum_{j=1}^k \text{Tr}(\rho(t) Z_j)]
[I_{\text{excitonic},i} = \kappa_e \text{Re}(E_i), \quad I_{\text{rhythm},i} = \sum_{f \in {4, 8, 12, 20, 40}} a_f \sin(2 \pi f t)]
Microtubule excitonic states ( E_i(t) \in \mathbb{C}^d ) evolve via:
[i \hbar \frac{dE_i}{dt} = (H_0 + \Delta - i \frac{G}{2}) E_i]
where ( H_0 = \text{diag}(0.5, \ldots, 0.5) ), ( \Delta \sim N(0, 0.1^2) ), ( G = \text{diag}(0.01, \ldots, 0.01) ) (OQTCF review; p. 47, Hameroff & Penrose).
2.3 Computational Neural Networks
The neural network is a multilayer graph ( N = (V, E, W) ), with weights ( W = { w_{ijl} } \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n \times L} ). Updates combine Hebbian and STDP learning, with Tse’s criterial causation via dynamic thresholds:
[\frac{d w_{ijl}}{dt} = \eta s_i s_j + \eta_{\text{STDP}} \exp\left(-\frac{|\Delta t_{ij}|}{\tau_{\text{STDP}}}\right) \text{sign}(\Delta t_{ij}) - \lambda_{\text{decay}} w_{ijl}]
where:

( s_i = 1_{V_i > \theta_i(t)} ), with dynamic threshold ( \theta_i(t) = -40 + \kappa_\theta \sum_j w_{ij} s_j ) (Tse, 2013).
( \Delta t_{ij} = t_i - t_j ): Spike time difference (p. 356, Green & Triffet).
( \eta, \eta_{\text{STDP}}, \tau_{\text{STDP}}, \lambda_{\text{decay}}, \kappa_\theta ): Parameters.

Memory, semantic, and apobetic updates:
[\frac{d M_{s,i}}{dt} = s_i \left( \frac{1}{m} \sum_k S_k(t) + \gamma_e E_i \right) - \alpha M_{s,i}]
[\frac{d M_{l,i}}{dt} = \text{Re}(\text{Tr}(\rho(t) M_{s,i} P_i)) - \alpha M_{l,i}]
[\frac{d S_{\text{sem},i}}{dt} = \eta_s \left( \sum_a \Psi_{i a} M_{l,i a} + \gamma_e \text{Re}(E_i) + \gamma_\Phi \text{ii}(s, s_{\text{effect}}) \right) - S_{\text{sem},i}]
[\frac{d P_i}{dt} = \beta_p S_{\text{sem},i} + \gamma_p \sum_{j \in N(i)} \omega_{ij} S_{\text{sem},j} + \gamma_e \text{Re}(E_i) + \gamma_\Phi \Phi(t) - \alpha P_i]
where ( \text{ii}(s, s_{\text{effect}}) = p(s_{\text{effect}} | s) \log \left( \frac{p(s_{\text{effect}} | s)}{p(s_{\text{effect}})} \right) ) (p. 12, Albantakis et al.; OQTCF review).
2.4 IIT Cause-Effect Structure
The substrate ( S ) is defined by its TPM ( \mathcal{T}_S = p(\bar{s} | s) ), capturing cause-effect power. The maximal substrate (complex) maximizes integrated information:
[\varphi_s = \min(\varphi_c, \varphi_e), \quad \varphi_c = \text{ii}(s, s_c^) - \text{ii}_{\text{MIP}}(s, s_c^), \quad \varphi_e = \text{ii}(s, s_e^) - \text{ii}_{\text{MIP}}(s, s_e^)]
The (\Phi)-structure is unfolded by identifying distinctions ( D(t) ) and relations ( R(t) ), with total structured information:
[\Phi(t) = \sum_{d \in D(t)} \varphi_d + \sum_{r \in R(t)} \varphi_r]
(p. 10, Albantakis et al.).
2.5 Quantum Dynamics: Orch OR and Bohm’s Quantum Potential
Microtubule quantum states evolve via:
[i \hbar \frac{d\rho}{dt} = [H(t), \rho(t)] + L \rho(t) L^\dagger - \frac{1}{2} { L^\dagger L, \rho(t) }]
where:

( H(t) = H_0 + \sum_{i,a} \Psi_{i a}(t) X_{i a} + \sum_i V_i(t) Z_i + Q_{\text{Bohm}}(t) ), with ( Q_{\text{Bohm}}(t) = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{\nabla^2 |\psi|}{|\psi|} ) (p. 666, Béjar Gallego).
( L = \sqrt{\gamma_d} I_k ), ( \gamma_d = 0.01 ).
Orch OR collapse at ( \tau \approx \hbar / E_G ):

[P(a) = \text{Tr}(\rho(t) P_a), \quad \rho(t) \to \frac{P_a \rho(t) P_a}{P(a)}]
Entanglement entropy:
[S(t) = -\text{Tr}(\rho(t) \log \rho(t))]
(p. 52, Hameroff & Penrose).
2.6 Field and Topological Dynamics
Field dynamics:
[\frac{d \Psi_{i a}}{dt} = D \sum_{j \in N(i)} \omega_{ij} (\Psi_{j a} - \Psi_{i a}) + \gamma \Psi_{i a} \left(1 - \frac{1}{K} \sum_{b=1}^d \Psi_{i b}^2\right) + \kappa s_i + \gamma_q \sum_{j=1}^k \text{Tr}(\rho(t) Z_j) + \sum_{j \in N(i)} \alpha_{ij} \Psi_{j a} \Psi_{i a} - \delta |\Psi_{i a}|^2 \left(2 + 0.5 \tanh\left(\frac{1}{|E|} \sum_{(u,v) \in E} \text{Ric}{uv}\right)\right) + \sigma \eta{i a} + \beta_{\text{EM}} E_{\text{EM},i a}]
Edge weights:
[\omega_{ij}(t + \Delta t) = \max\left( \omega_{ij}(t) + 0.1 \left( \sum_{a=1}^d \Psi_{i a} \Psi_{j a} + s_i s_j - 0.1 \omega_{ij} - 0.02 \text{Ric}{ij} \right), \omega{\text{threshold}} \right)]
Betti numbers ( \beta_k(t) = \dim H_k(S(t), \mathbb{R}) ) (p. 356, Green & Triffet).
2.7 Decision-Making and Free Will
Decisions:
[P(d | \rho(t), M_r, V(t)) = \text{softmax}d \left( s_i(t) + \gamma_q \sum{j=1}^k \text{Tr}(\rho(t) Z_j) + \frac{1}{d} \sum_{a=1}^d M_{r,i a}(t) \right)]
[D(t) = \arg \max_d P(d | \rho(t), M_r, V(t))]
Criterial causation and Orch OR enable free will (Tse, 2013; p. 674, Béjar Gallego).
2.8 Metastability
Metastability is detected via:
[\lambda_{\text{max}} = \lim_{T \to \infty} \frac{1}{T} \int_0^T \log \left( \max\left( \left| \frac{dV(t)}{dt} \right|, 10^{-10} \right) \right) dt]
If ( |\lambda_{\text{max}}| < 0.1 ), noise amplifies (p. 356, Green & Triffet).
3. Computational Model: P System-Based Quantal Turing Machine
The model is a hybrid P system and quantal Turing machine:
[\Pi = (O, C, \mu, w_1, \ldots, w_m, R_1, \ldots, R_m, i_0)]

Objects (( O )): ( V_i, s_i, w_{ijl}, M_{s,i}, M_{l,i}, S_{\text{sem},i}, P_i, E_i, \Psi_{i a}, \rho, \mathcal{T}_S ).
Catalysts (( C )): Attentional promoters ( C_A ) (Tse, 2013).
Membrane Structure (( \mu )): Simplicial complex ( S(t) ), with membranes for sensory (V1), binding (V4), and decision-making (frontoparietal) regions.
Rules (( R_i )): Chained rules for neural, excitonic, quantum, field, topological, and IIT updates.
Output Region (( i_0 )): Outputs ( C(t) ), (\Phi)-structure.

   - R(t) = Σ_f a_f sin(2π f t)

2. For t = 0 to T, step Δt:
   a. Update Topology (P System):
      - Rule: ω_ij → ω_ij' | Ψ_i Ψ_j s_i s_j
      - Compute Ric_ij(t), ω_ij(t + Δt), β_k(t)

   f. Update Field Dynamics:
      - Rule: Ψ_i a → Ψ_i a'

      - Rule: (M_r ρ → P_d, P_d → D)
