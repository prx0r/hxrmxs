Abstract
The subjective experience of musical pleasure, or valence, has long eluded a formal scientific explanation. This thesis proposes and provides a validation protocol for a novel framework that directly links the objective mathematical structure of music to the phenomenology of affective experience. The core hypothesis is a direct application of the Symmetry Theory of Valence (STV), which posits that the valence of a conscious state is isomorphic to the mathematical symmetry of its underlying neural representation. We bridge this theory to the domain of music by leveraging Dmitri Tymoczko's geometric music theory, which provides a formal method for quantifying the intrinsic symmetry of harmonic structures.

The proposed STV-Geometric Music Framework establishes a direct, testable empirical pathway: Musical Geometric Symmetry → Neural Symmetry Patterns (EEG) → Subjective Valence. This thesis outlines a multi-phase experimental protocol to validate this pathway. First, we define a computational method, the TymoczkoSymmetryAnalyzer, to derive a quantitative geometric_symmetry_index for any given chord progression. Second, we propose a tiered approach for quantifying the corresponding symmetry of neural activity from EEG data, including a "gold standard" method based on the Connectome-Specific Harmonic Wave (CSHW) framework and a computationally feasible proxy based on Inter-Channel Phase Coherence. Finally, we detail a systematic experimental design using controlled musical stimuli to test the core prediction: that musical stimuli with higher geometric symmetry will induce measurably more symmetric neural states, which in turn will be reported as having higher subjective valence. A successful validation would provide the first strong empirical evidence for STV in a complex aesthetic domain and establish a new, mathematically-grounded paradigm for the science of musical emotion.

Chapter 1: Theoretical Foundation
1.1. The Explanatory Gap in Musical Aesthetics
While the power of music to evoke emotion is a universal human experience, scientific models have struggled to bridge the gap between objective acoustic properties and subjective phenomenal character. Theories based on cultural association or simple psychoacoustic features fail to explain the profound sense of order, elegance, and pleasure derived from complex harmonic progressions. This research addresses this gap by proposing that the aesthetics of music are grounded in fundamental mathematical and physical principles of information and symmetry processing in the brain.

1.2. The Three Pillars of the Framework
This thesis integrates three distinct but deeply complementary theoretical pillars:

Geometric Music Theory (Tymoczko): Tymoczko's work provides a formal, objective foundation for quantifying the structure of musical harmony. It posits that Western tonal music is constrained by five core properties (conjunct melodic motion, acoustic consonance, harmonic consistency, limited macroharmony, and centricity). These properties allow music to be represented in geometric spaces—typically orbifolds—where points are chords and distances represent voice-leading efficiency. Crucially, this framework allows us to compute the symmetry group of a harmonic object, providing a precise, quantitative measure of its intrinsic geometric order.

The Symmetry Theory of Valence (STV): STV proposes a radical and elegant solution to the "hard problem" of consciousness, at least for the dimension of valence. Its central hypothesis is that the pleasantness of an experience is identical to the mathematical symmetry of the information processing that realizes it. A state feels good because its neural representation is physically symmetric, harmonious, and ordered. Dissonance and suffering correspond to asymmetric, disordered, and noisy neural states.

Predictive Processing & Neural Annealing: While STV describes the nature of the state, predictive processing describes the process of reaching it. Dissonant or surprising musical events "heat" the neural system by injecting prediction error (free energy), creating a disordered, high-temperature state. A consonant resolution allows the system to efficiently "cool" or anneal into a low-energy, stable, and highly symmetric final configuration. The pleasure of music is therefore the pleasure of successful neural annealing into a symmetric state.

1.3. The Core Empirical Bridge
The novelty of this framework is the direct bridge it creates: Tymoczko's theory gives us a way to measure the objective symmetry of the stimulus. STV gives us a hypothesis about the subjective experience of valence. EEG gives us access to the intermediate neural processing. We can therefore test the entire causal chain: does the symmetry of the music cause symmetry in the brain, and does that symmetry in the brain cause the feeling of pleasure?

class TymoczkoSymmetryAnalyzer:
    """
    A computational tool to quantify the geometric and structural
    symmetry of a given chord progression based on geometric music theory.
    """
    def __init__(self):
        # These would be complex modules implementing Tymoczko's mathematics.
        self.voice_leading_space = VoiceLeadingOrbifold()
        self.consonance_calculator = AcousticConsonanceMetric()
        self.macroharmonic_analyzer = ScalarTranspositionSpace()

    def quantify_geometric_symmetry(self, chord_progression):
        """
        Extracts a composite symmetry index from a musical passage.
        """
        # 1. Map chord progression to a sequence of points in a geometric space.
        chord_points = self.voice_leading_space.map_chords(chord_progression)
        
        # 2. Calculate the size of the symmetry group for the chord set.
        # This measures how many transformations (e.g., transposition, inversion)
        # leave the set of chords invariant.
        symmetry_operations = self.compute_symmetry_group(chord_points)
        
        # 3. Quantify voice-leading efficiency, a measure of path smoothness.
        efficiency_score = self.measure_voice_leading_efficiency(chord_points)
        
        # 4. Analyze harmonic consistency within the progression.
        consistency_metric = self.assess_harmonic_consistency(chord_progression)
        
        # 5. Combine these into a single, objective index of musical symmetry.
        geometric_symmetry_index = (len(symmetry_operations) * 0.5 + 
                                   efficiency_score * 0.3 + 
                                   consistency_metric * 0.2)
        
        return {
            'symmetry_group_size': len(symmetry_operations),
            'voice_leading_efficiency': efficiency_score,
            'harmonic_consistency': consistency_metric,
            'geometric_symmetry_index': geometric_symmetry_index
        }

Success Criterion: A significant positive correlation (r > 0.4, p < 0.01), demonstrating that as the brain state becomes more symmetric, the experience becomes more pleasant.
